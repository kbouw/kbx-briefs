## AI/ML

### 1. Why hasn't longer-horizon training slowed AI progress?

AI progress hasn't slowed despite longer training horizons because training efficiency gains from bug fixes (like GPT-4's initial FP16 summation error) can outweigh computational overhead, plus capability improvements often come from non-intelligence factors like persistence and working memory rather than raw FLOP increases. Human inability to judge superintelligence makes progress measurement increasingly unreliable as models approach human-level performance.

### 3. Vibe coding and agentic engineering are getting closer than I'd like

Simon Willison admits that reliable AI coding tools like Claude Code have blurred his distinction between 'vibe coding' and professional engineering—he's now shipping production code without reviewing every line because AI-generated straightforward tasks (JSON APIs, SQL queries) consistently work correctly. This creates a normalization of deviance risk where increased trust from repeated success could lead to getting burned when the AI fails on more complex tasks.
