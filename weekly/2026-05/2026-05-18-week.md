## Executive Summary

AI's economic impact reached an inflection point this week, with coding agents crossing from "often-work" to "mostly-work" quality while simultaneously triggering a memory shortage that will reprice consumer electronics globally. The convergence of AI capability breakthroughs and infrastructure constraints signals we're entering a new phase where AI deployment is limited more by hardware economics than software capabilities.

## Top Trends

**AI Infrastructure Economics Are Breaking**
- Evidence: SpaceX's $1.25 billion monthly cloud deal with Anthropic, Gemini 3.5 Flash pricing at 3x predecessor costs, AI consuming 20% of memory manufacturers' wafer capacity vs 2% previously
- Why it matters: The gap between AI capability and deployment cost is widening rapidly, forcing companies to test price tolerance limits while hardware shortages cascade to consumer electronics

**Surveillance Infrastructure Expansion**
- Evidence: FBI seeking nationwide license plate reader access, Seattle Shield intelligence-sharing network, Tesla's daily wastewater discharge monitoring
- Why it matters: Multiple vectors of surveillance are converging into comprehensive tracking capabilities, fundamentally altering privacy expectations across transportation and digital spaces

**Platform Consolidation and Fragmentation**
- Evidence: Google deprecating open-source Gemini CLI for closed Antigravity, implementing anti-web policies, 340+ news outlets blocking Internet Archive
- Why it matters: Major platforms are simultaneously consolidating control while fragmenting open standards, creating walled gardens that undermine web openness

**AI Agent Maturation**
- Evidence: Coding agents reaching "mostly-work" quality, Datasette Agent for conversational database queries, OpenClaw causing Mac Mini shortages
- Why it matters: AI agents are transitioning from experimental tools to daily-driver applications, fundamentally changing how humans interact with software and data

## Developing Stories

**AI Model Competition Intensification**: The "best" model crown changed hands five times between Claude, GPT, and Gemini variants in six months, with Google aggressively pricing Gemini 3.5 Flash despite 3x cost increases. This suggests model differentiation is becoming more nuanced and expensive.

**Memory Supply Chain Crisis**: HBM demand requiring 3x more wafer capacity per gigabyte is already impacting sub-$100 smartphones in developing markets, with broader consumer electronics repricing inevitable over the next several years.

**Mathematical AI Breakthroughs**: OpenAI models disproving discrete geometry conjectures could accelerate automated theorem proving across scientific research.

**Regulatory Precedents**: Minnesota's prediction market ban and the FTC's "Active Listening" settlement may signal broader crackdowns on emerging tech business models.
