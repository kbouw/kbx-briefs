## AI/ML

### 1. 🔴 DeepSeek V4 - almost on the frontier, a fraction of the price

DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T parameters) and V4-Flash (284B parameters) deliver near-frontier performance at dramatically lower costs—Flash costs $0.14/$0.28 per million tokens vs GPT-5.4 Nano's $0.20/$1.25, while achieving 90% efficiency gains over DeepSeek V3.2 for long contexts. The models use MIT licensing and represent the largest open weights release to date.

### 2. An update on recent Claude Code quality reports

Anthropic's postmortem reveals Claude Code's quality issues weren't model problems but harness bugs—including one that cleared context memory every turn in resumed sessions instead of just once, making Claude seem forgetful. This particularly impacts long-running agentic workflows where sessions are frequently paused and resumed.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/24/recent-claude-code-quality-reports/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** Claude Code, prompt-engineering, agentic systems

## Industry

### 3. Software engineering may no longer be a lifetime career

Software engineers may need to treat their careers like pro athletes—maximizing earnings in a 15-year window before AI-assisted coding skills atrophy makes them unemployable, similar to how carpenters who refuse power tools get outcompeted. The author argues engineers should plan for career transitions rather than avoid AI tools that provide short-term competitive advantage.
