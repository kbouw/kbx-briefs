## AI/ML

### 1. Giving LLMs a personality is just good engineering

AI systems need human-like personalities not as marketing gimmicks but as core engineering requirements—base models trained on raw data are chaotic and unusable until post-training gives them personalities that constrain outputs to helpful rather than harmful regions of their capability space. This explains why making AI systems "less human" would likely make them less capable, since personality is the technical mechanism that makes LLMs useful at all.

**Source:** [Sean Goedecke](https://seangoedecke.com/giving-llms-a-personality/)
**Tags:** LLM training, AI personality, post-training, base models

### 2. Show HN: I built a sub-500ms latency voice agent from scratch

Nick Tikhonov achieved 400ms end-to-end voice agent latency by treating voice as a turn-taking problem requiring semantic end-of-turn detection rather than just VAD, with Groq's 80ms time-to-first-token being the biggest performance win. The key insight: streaming STT→LLM→TTS pipelines with instant barge-in cancellation and geographic colocation matter more than prompt engineering for natural conversation flow.

**Source:** [Hacker News Best](https://www.ntik.me/posts/voice-agent)
**Tags:** voice agents, STT, TTS, Groq
