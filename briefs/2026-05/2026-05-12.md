# Daily Intelligence Brief — May 12, 2026

## AI/ML

### 1. 🔴 Thinking Machines and interaction models

Thinking Machines released their first AI model after $2B funding—a fully-duplex voice+video system that delegates complex reasoning to background models while maintaining real-time 200ms conversation turns. The key breakthrough is scaling this architecture to DeepSeek V4-Flash size (40x larger than previous fully-duplex models), enabling genuine face-to-face AI conversations that can interrupt and respond to facial expressions.

**Source:** [Sean Goedecke](https://seangoedecke.com/interaction-models/)

### 3. Your AI Use Is Breaking My Brain

Jason Koebler coins "Zombie Internet" to describe the exhausting reality of navigating AI-human hybrid content where people use AI tools to interact with other humans, creating a more insidious problem than pure bot networks—it's mentally draining to filter and starting to corrupt human writing styles.

### 4. Interaction Models

Thinking Machines outlines four core interaction models for AI systems: command-based (direct instructions), conversational (natural dialogue), assistant-based (proactive help), and collaborative (shared problem-solving), arguing that choosing the right model depends on user expertise, task complexity, and desired control level.

**Source:** [Hacker News Best](https://thinkingmachines.ai/blog/interaction-models/)
**Tags:** interaction design, AI interfaces, conversational AI, UX patterns

### 5. Quoting James Shore

James Shore argues AI coding agents only provide value if they reduce maintenance costs by the exact inverse of productivity gains—if you code 2x faster, you need 50% lower maintenance costs, or you'll quadruple total costs. The math shows temporary speed boosts become permanent debt traps without proportional maintenance reduction.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/11/james-shore/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** AI coding agents, code maintenance, LLMs, developer productivity

## Industry

### 2. 🔴 Thoughts on GitLab's workforce reduction" and "structural and strategic decisions"

GitLab is cutting workforce across 30% of its 60-country footprint and flattening management by up to 3 layers while doubling R&D teams to 60 smaller units, betting that agentic engineering will multiply software demand despite stock dropping from $52 to $26. They're removing 'Diversity' from core values while predicting developer platform pricing will jump from tens to thousands per user monthly as AI agents collapse software production costs.
