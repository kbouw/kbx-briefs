### 1. Weird projects I shipped with AI

Developer demonstrates AI's real impact through 6 shipped side projects including Skifreedle (SkiFree + Wordle), auto-generated Anki cards, and an infinite wiki—projects that wouldn't have existed without LLMs reducing implementation friction. Shows AI enables prolific experimentation: trying 15-20 visual themes instead of 2-3, tackling complex scraping tasks, and actually deploying with databases/payments rather than abandoning in GitHub repos.

### 2. The solution might be cancelling my AI subscription

David Wilson warns that AI coding agents create a "thermonuclear ADHD amplifier" effect, generating dozens of instantly-abandoned projects that provide cheap dopamine hits but no real value. However, ADHD developers in HN comments report the opposite—finally finishing projects because agents help them build working solutions before losing interest.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/31/the-solution-might-be-cancelling-my-ai-subscription/#atom-everything)
