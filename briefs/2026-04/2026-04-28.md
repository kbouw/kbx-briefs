# Daily Intelligence Brief — April 28, 2026

## AI/ML

### 1. 🔴 Introducing talkie: a 13B vintage language model from 1930

Researchers released talkie, a 13B parameter LLM trained exclusively on pre-1931 text (260B tokens), creating the first major "vegan" language model with entirely copyright-free training data. The model enables fascinating experiments like testing whether AI trained to 1911 could independently discover General Relativity, though the chat version required modern LLM assistance for fine-tuning.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/28/talkie/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** vintage LLMs, copyright-free training, historical text models, talkie

### 2. 🔴 Tracking the history of the now-deceased OpenAI Microsoft AGI clause

Microsoft's AGI clause with OpenAI—which would have voided Microsoft's commercial IP rights once AGI is achieved—has been officially eliminated in their latest partnership agreement, with Microsoft now getting non-exclusive licensing through 2032 regardless of AGI development. The clause evolved from a vague definition to requiring $100 billion in profits (2024) to independent expert panel verification (2025) before being scrapped entirely in 2026.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/27/now-deceased-agi-clause/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** OpenAI, Microsoft, AGI, licensing

### 3. 🔴 microsoft/VibeVoice

Microsoft's new VibeVoice speech-to-text model processes audio 6.8x faster than real-time on M5 Max (8m45s for 1hr audio) with built-in speaker diarization, delivering timestamped transcripts that automatically identify different speakers—though limited to 1-hour chunks and requiring 30GB+ RAM.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/27/vibevoice/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** VibeVoice, speech-to-text, MLX, speaker diarization
