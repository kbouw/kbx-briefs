### 1. AI GPUs probably live longer than three years

Analysis of GPU lifespan data from Oak Ridge's Titan supercomputer shows 95% survival at 3 years and 90%+ at 6 years for properly cooled units, contradicting viral claims that AI GPUs only last 1-3 years. The original "three years at most" figure traces to an anonymous paid interview designed to sound authoritative, while Google reports 8-year-old TPUs still running at 100% utilization.

**Source:** [Sean Goedecke](https://seangoedecke.com/ai-gpus-live-longer-than-three-years/)
**Tags:** GPU infrastructure, AI datacenter economics, inference costs

### 2. Why AI hasn’t replaced software engineers, and won’t

Analysis of New York's first-year WARN Act data shows zero of 160+ companies cited AI for layoffs, while research reveals software engineering's core bottlenecks aren't coding speed but deciding what to build, verifying deliverables, and deep contextual understanding of codebases and business needs. AI accelerates typing but can't replace the human judgment and domain expertise that drive engineering decisions.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/14/why-ai-hasnt-replaced-software-engineers/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** AI displacement, software engineering, WARN Act
