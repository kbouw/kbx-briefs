# Daily Intelligence Brief — June 11, 2026

## AI/ML

### 1. 🔴 DiffusionGemma

Google open-sourced DiffusionGemma-26B under Apache 2.0 license, delivering 500+ tokens/second text-to-image generation via NVIDIA's free NIM API. This marks the return of Google's experimental Gemini Diffusion research as a production-ready open model that developers can actually use and modify.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/10/diffusiongemma/#atom-everything)
**Tags:** DiffusionGemma, text-to-image generation, Apache 2.0, NVIDIA NIM

### 2. Quoting Jeremy Howard

Jeremy Howard proposes that labs with top AI models should be barred from using them for frontier research while giving everyone else access, arguing this prevents recursive self-improvement without creating power imbalances. He criticizes Anthropic for doing the opposite—using Claude for their own research while restricting others' access.

**Source:** [Simon Willison's Weblog](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/10/jeremy-howard/#atom-everything)
